DeckBuilding

Deckbuilding is one of the most important parts of Star Wars Unlimited.

Deckbuilding Rules

To make a premier deck (the primary game mode), you need to combine a minimum of 50 cards together with no more than three copies of any given card. Additionally, you may bring a sideboard of no more than 10 cards. Between rounds, you are allowed to swap cards between the main and side boards so long as you follow the minimum 50/maximum 10 rules.

Copies Rule

A copy of a card is a card that shares that shares the same name and properties as another. This means that the convention exclusive Kylo Ren is a copy of normal Kylo Ren because they both share the same stats, abilities, and name even if they do look different. However, Set 2 Maul and Set 3 Maul are completely different and therefore are not considered "copies" of each other.

Packs

Like all TCGs, Star Wars Unlimited features boxes and packs to open. All display boxes come with 24 packs of 16 cards. At minimum, you will find:

  • 1 leader
  • 1 base
  • 9 commons
  • 3 uncommons
  • 1 rare
  • 1 foil of any rarity

Additionally, all cards have a certain chance of being a borderless, hyperspace card.

Set 1: Sparks of Rebellion

The very first set of the game opens up by focusing on the era that started it all: the era of the Galatic Civil War. This set introduces a very well-rounded set with leaders that represent their aspects well. This set also has smaller themes focusing on:

  • Rebels
  • Inferno Squad
  • Rogue One

Set 2: Shadows of the Galaxy

The sequel to Sparks of Rebellion focuses on the underworld of Star Wars and on the era following the Civil War: The Mandaloreon Era. Naturally, there's also a lot of focus on Mandaloreons (particularly in the Vigilance/Aggression aspects). With the focus on the Underworld, we are naturally introduced to Bounty, Smuggle, and capture mechanics.

  • Solo
  • Bad Batch
  • The Book of Boba Fett
  • The Era of Resistance

Set 3: Twilight of the Republic

The third set and final set for 2024 is another core set, this time focusing on the era of the Clone Wars. We get introduced to Coordinate and Exploit. This set is also the first set to introduce us to token units, who are capable of infinitely spawning into the game.

Common Deck Types

Aggro

Aggro decks tend to want to move fast. One of the most popular aggro decks is Energy Conversion Lab (ECL) Sabine Wren, who deploys quickly and uses ECL to ECL out heavy cards like Wrecker and Poe Dameron.

Midrange

Midrange decks are pretty average decks that deploy heavier units than aggro while being faster than long-range control decks.

Control

Control decks want to maintain control of the board. Control decks typically win by stalling the opponent until they run out of big plays. Then, the control deck player finishes off their opponent with their own huge plays. Control decks will typically use Vigilance (the card) to mill (or discard cards from) the opponent's deck. One of my favorite examples of a good control deck is Vigilance Rey who uses her Restore 3 to spam healing into her base while buffing weaker units.

Tempo

Tempo decks are decks that want to out-value their opponent's plays. They typically do so by returning enemy units back to hand or playing higher value units for shorter term gain. Good examples of this are leaders like Han Solo and Young Han.

Ramp

Ramp Decks typically want to play cards like Resupply. The goal of ramp decks are to get to expensive cards as quickly as possible.
